Pillars

Three pillars of impact.

The Signals Institute operates at three mutually reinforcing levels: open community engagement, youth exposure, and veteran transition—each designed to compound the others over time.

01

Engagement

Youth · Veterans · Community

Open access, no prerequisites. Forums, workshops, and researcher dialogues that make technical work legible to the whole community.

  • Monthly recurring forums
  • Researcher Q&A in community spaces
  • Plain-language pathways to technical careers
02

Exposure

Youth · Veterans · Community

Hands-on discovery experiences that connect people to research and technical careers—before they learn the vocabulary.

  • Ages 10–18, two-track model
  • Workshops, camps, internships
  • Mentorship + portfolio outputs
03

Transition

Youth · Veterans · Community

Structured pathways moving youth into careers, veterans into civilian roles, and the broader community into technical opportunity.

  • SkillBridge-aligned where eligible
  • Employer pipeline + placement support
  • Outcomes tied to wages + durable roles
Engagement

Community access is the front door.

Engagement is how we normalize technical opportunity in everyday spaces. We run open forums, hands-on workshops, and partner-hosted sessions that make research and engineering careers visible, understandable, and attainable.

Forums

Researcher Dialogues

Workshops

Signals, AI & Tools

Partners

Local hosting network

How it works

A repeatable cadence that builds trust.

We design engagement as an operating system: consistent events, predictable pathways, and measurable follow-through. Each event points participants to the next step—youth programs, veteran transition, or continued community learning.

01

Host

Partners provide space (library, school, city facility). We bring facilitators and curriculum.

02

Deliver

Workshops are hands-on and simple: radios, signals, software tools, and real career narratives.

03

Convert to pathways

Participants exit with a clear next step: program track, intake, mentorship, or partner referral.

Youth Programs

Two distinct tracks calibrated to developmental stage—designed to turn curiosity into confidence, and confidence into technical direction.

Track A — Exposure · Ages 10–14

Hands-on discovery

  • Researcher workshop series (monthly)
  • Summer Discovery Camp (2-week intensive)
  • School & community workshops (demand-driven)
Track B — Career Development · Ages 14–18

Structured workforce preparation

  • After-school technical program (semester cohort)
  • Summer Technical Intensive (project-based)
  • Science fair mentorship (year-round)
  • Internships + portfolio outputs

Transition
